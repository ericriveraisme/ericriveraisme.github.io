01 Executive Summary

In the current cyber insurance landscape, businesses are required to provide technical attestations regarding their security controls. Traditional audits often involve invasive access to sensitive Data/PII, creating a secondary liability for the business. The D3 Zero-Knowledge Extraction Engine solves this by utilizing a "Privacy-First" protocol that extracts system metadata while ensuring no Personally Identifiable Information (PII) ever leaves the client's network.

02 The Privacy-Liability Paradox

Standard IT audits often require full administrative access, exposing the auditor to sensitive records (HIPAA/ePHI, financial data, PII). This creates two primary risks:

Compliance Breach

The act of auditing creates a new "data transfer" event that must be governed and logged under HIPAA/PIPA.

Theft Risk

If an auditor's device is compromised, the client's raw, unencrypted PII is exposed to the threat actor.

03 The D3 Protocol

"Technical Proof of Control without Exposure of Content."

A

Metadata Extraction

The engine utilizes the D3 SQL Diagnostics Suite to query server-level metadata. It ignores user-table content entirely, focusing on Database Integrity, Backup Consistency, and Encryption Status.

B

SHA256 Identity Anonymization

To audit Active Directory, the engine utilizes a proprietary PowerShell hashing function. Every username is passed through a SHA256 salted hash at the point of extraction, creating a "PrivacyID." Only the client onsite can re-map these IDs.

C

Air-Gapped Analysis

Data is extracted to a BitLocker-encrypted hardware token. The analysis is performed on a dedicated, air-gapped forensic workstation. No client data is ever uploaded to public cloud providers.
